In an online ticket booking system, there are several key business entities involved. Here are some common entities:
User: Users can register, log in, browse events, book tickets, make payments, 
      and manage their profiles.
	
Event: Represents a specific happening or occurrence for which tickets can be booked. 
       Events can include movies, concerts, sports matches, etc. 
       Each event has attributes such as a name, date, time, venue, category, and availability status.
	
Ticket: Tickets are associated with events and have attributes such as a unique identifier,
        seat number, price, and booking status.
	
Booking: A booking is associated with a user and contains details such as the booked tickets, 
        total price, booking date, and status (e.g., confirmed, canceled).
	
Payment: Represents the financial transaction made by a user to purchase tickets.
         It includes payment details such as the payment method (credit card, debit card, net banking), amount, transaction ID, and payment status.
	
Venue:  A venue can have attributes such as a name, address, seating layout, capacity, 
        and facilities.
